Overview

What AuraPredict is

AuraPredict is a testnet prediction market venue for binary questions. Traders stake Arc native USDC on YES or NO, creators resolve markets after close, and winners claim payout from the contract. The product is designed to make public forecasting measurable: every wallet can build history, PNL, win rate, Aura Points, and creator reputation.

Onchain trading

Market creation, staking, resolution, dispute, stale-dispute cancel, and claim are contract interactions.

Fast read layer

The indexer reads Arc events and exposes market stats, activity, wallet-searchable history, and leaderboard data.

Social layer

Profiles, comments, evidence links, follows, share links, and reputation make forecasting public.

Aura Agent

AI drafts markets, checks duplicate risk, summarizes evidence, and creates resolution receipts.

AI receipts

The indexer can calculate a proposed outcome off-chain while contract settlement stays unchanged.

Users

Use AuraPredict

  1. Open app.aurapredict.xyz.
  2. Connect MetaMask, Rabby, OKX, Zerion, or another injected EVM wallet.
  3. Switch to Arc Testnet when prompted, or add the network manually with the settings above.
  4. Fund the wallet with Arc Testnet USDC from the Circle faucet.
  5. Open a market, choose YES or NO, enter the USDC amount, confirm the wallet transaction.
  6. Track the market in your profile. If your position wins after finalization, claim payout.

Profile data

  • Wallet display name and public profile link.
  • Available USDC balance on Arc Testnet.
  • Markets participated and markets created.
  • Resolved wins, losses, PNL, win streak, and claimable payout.

Notifications

  • Markets that need creator resolution.
  • Results ready to finalize.
  • Stale disputes that can be canceled to refund both sides.
  • Claims available after settlement.
  • Transaction confirmation links to Arcscan.
Use a testnet-only wallet. AuraPredict is not a mainnet financial product.
Creators

Create clear markets

Question checklist

  • Use one clear YES/NO outcome.
  • Include exact event boundary and UTC close time.
  • Define acceptable sources before launch.
  • Avoid subjective words such as successful, major, best, or viral.
  • Review duplicate-risk suggestions before publishing.

Resolution checklist

  • Attach evidence links before proposing a result.
  • Run AI resolution when evidence is ready to create an auditable off-chain receipt.
  • Use Cancel only when the market cannot be fairly resolved.
  • Wait through the dispute window before treating the result as final.
  • Keep official sources and screenshots for contested results.
Contract

Smart contract behavior

1

Create

Creator opens a market, sets category and close time, locks the creator bond, and pays any configured creation fee.

2

Trade

Users stake native USDC on YES or NO before the market close time.

3

Propose

Creator, owner, or the configured resolution authority proposes YES, NO, or Cancel after close, optionally using an AI receipt.

4

Dispute

Only users with a position in that market can dispute during the dispute window with a dispute bond.

5

Stale dispute

If a dispute is not finalized after the grace period, anyone can cancel it and refund users.

6

Claim

After finalization, winners claim payout or both sides refund if canceled.

AI resolution

AI outcome calculation without changing the contract

AuraPredict V2 keeps AI calculation off-chain. The indexer runs an AI review committee, stores a public resolution receipt, and leaves final settlement to the creator, owner, or future resolution authority flow on the contract.

1

Collect evidence

Creators and users attach source links, notes, and resolution context to the market.

2

Run reviewers

The indexer asks three AI reviewer roles to return YES, NO, Cancel, or insufficient evidence.

3

Create receipt

The consensus, confidence, reasoning, risks, evidence, and immutable decision hash are stored off-chain.

4

Propose result

An admin can review the receipt, then the resolver, owner, or configured authority signs the normal contract transaction.

Public read endpoint

GET /api/resolutions/:marketId

The app uses this endpoint to show the AI receipt panel on a market detail page.

Admin run endpoint

POST /api/resolutions/:marketId/run
Authorization: Bearer your_admin_token

Admin runs require AURA_RESOLUTION_ADMIN_TOKEN. Use force: true only when rerunning after better evidence.

AI providerAI_PROVIDER=gemini and GEMINI_API_KEY
Manual modeReceipt only; human reviews and signs the result proposal.
Auto-runAURA_RESOLUTION_AUTO_RUN=1 creates the first receipt for closed unresolved markets.
Auto-proposeAURA_RESOLUTION_AUTO_PROPOSE=1 requires a resolver, owner, or authority private key.
AI receipts are decision support, not a trustless oracle. The contract still controls settlement, users can still dispute, and private resolver or authority keys must stay outside GitHub and Vercel logs.
Indexer

Indexer and data flow

The frontend can read directly from Arc RPC, but production UX is smoother with the AuraPredict indexer. The indexer reads contract events, stores JSON snapshots, and serves fast read endpoints for stats, market rows, activity, leaderboard views, social data, and AI resolution receipts.

Current production indexer endpoint: https://aurapredict-indexer.onrender.com

Security

Security model and limitations

Wallet custody

AuraPredict never receives private keys. Every transaction is confirmed in the user's wallet.

Testnet only

The current deployment is for Arc Testnet validation and should not be treated as audited mainnet infrastructure.

AI limitation

Aura Agent and AI receipts assist research and outcome calculation. They are not a trustless oracle.

Resolution risk

Creator or authority-led resolution requires clear criteria, evidence, and dispute review to avoid ambiguous settlement.

Admin keys

AI run tokens, Gemini keys, and optional resolver private keys must stay in private deployment secrets.

Before mainnet, AuraPredict needs deeper contract review, durable social data storage, stronger oracle/dispute design, and operational monitoring.
